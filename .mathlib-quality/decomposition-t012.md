## 1. Source confirmation (Miyake §4.6 + DS §5.6) — VERBATIM

### Miyake's old space `S_k^♭(N,χ)` — definition (p. 162, OCR `docs/Toshitsune Miyake.pdf` p. 171)

> "We denote by 𝒮ₖ^♭(N,χ) the subspace of 𝒮ₖ(N,χ) generated by the set
> `⋃_M ⋃_l {f(lz) | f(z) ∈ 𝒮ₖ^♯(M,χ)}`. Here M runs over all positive integers such that
> m_χ | M, M | N, and M ≠ N; l runs over all positive divisors of N/M (including both 1 and
> N/M); m_χ is the conductor of χ. In other words, 𝒮ₖ^♭(N,χ) is the subspace of 𝒮ₖ(N,χ)
> generated by cusp forms essentially of lower levels."

Lean ↔ source: `cuspFormsOldChar` (`StrongMultiplicityOneFull.lean:216`) = `Submodule.span` of
`{V_l(g) | g ∈ cuspFormsNew M k, m_χ ∣ M, M ≠ N, l·M = N}`. **MATCH on generators (V_l of NEW
spaces).** Two divergences flagged below: (a) Miyake takes `l | N/M` (all divisors, incl. l=1
and l=N/M) but the Lean def fixes `l·M = N` (only l = N/M) — see ATTACK-l (L0); (b) Miyake's
`𝒮ₖ^♯(M,χ)` is the χ-NEW space, the Lean def uses char-agnostic `cuspFormsNew M k` — see ATTACK-χ.

### Miyake Lemma 4.6.9 (p. 162) — VERBATIM

> "(1) If χ is a primitive Dirichlet character of conductor N, then 𝒮ₖ^♯(N,χ) = 𝒮ₖ(N,χ).
> (2) If m_χ | M, M | N and M ≠ N, then 𝒮ₖ^♯(M,χ) ⊆ 𝒮ₖ^♭(N,χ).
> (3) 𝒮ₖ^♭(N,χ) is generated by the set `⋃_M ⋃_l {f(lz) | f(z) ∈ 𝒮ₖ^♭(M,χ)}`. Here M runs
> over all positive integers such that m_χ | M and M | N; l runs over all positive divisors
> of N/M (including both 1 and N/M)."

**This is the recursion.** 4.6.9(3) says the old space at level N is generated by V_l-images of
(new/old) spaces at proper-divisor levels M (m_χ | M | N). It is Miyake's own statement that the
old space is **recursively** built from lower levels. *(OCR superscript on `𝒮ₖ^?(M,χ)` in (3) is
illegible — `9'°` could be ♯ or ♭; the definition (quoted above) is unambiguous that the
GENERATORS are V_l of the NEW spaces ♯, and the recursion folds in the old pieces. Either reading
yields the same span, since at level M: old(M) = Σ V(♯ at <M) by induction, so V_l(♭(M)) and
V_l(♯(M)) generate the same total over all M.)*

### Miyake Theorem 4.6.8 — the induction template (p. 159-162; (4.6.14) proof on p. 162)

The p. 162 OCR shows the *engine* Miyake uses for the whole old/new theory:

> "by the induction assumption, we obtain that `f(z) − fₚ(pz) = Σ_q f_M(q)(l_q z) ∈ 𝒮ₖ(N/q, χ)`,
> where q runs over all prime factors of l. This completes the proof of (2). If f(z) is a cusp
> form, then the modular forms which appear in the proof can be taken as cusp forms."

So Miyake proves 4.6.8(2) (and the surrounding old/new structure) **"by induction"** on the
factorisation of the level-raising index l, peeling one prime factor q at a time and descending

descend to N/p, fold the deeper old pieces back through V_l-composition).

## 3. Induction structure (the spine)

**Induction variable:** `N` itself, via `Nat.strong_induction_on` (strong induction on the level).
Equivalently Ω(N) = number of prime factors with multiplicity; strong induction on N is cleaner in
Lean (no need to compute Ω). Mirrors Miyake 4.6.8's "induction on the factors of l" (p. 162) and
DS Exercise 5.6.2's divisor-recursion.

**Statement of the induction (the workhorse, char-fixed at every level):**

```lean
theorem cuspFormsOld_inf_charSpace_le_cuspFormsOldChar_aux
    (N : ℕ) [NeZero N] (k : ℤ) (χ : DirichletCharacter ℂ N) :
    cuspFormsOld N k ⊓ cuspFormCharSpace k χ.toUnitHom ≤
      cuspFormsOldChar N k χ.toUnitHom χ.conductor
-- proven by strong induction on N.
```

**Base case (N = 1, or more generally `cuspFormsOld N k = ⊥`).** When `N` has no proper divisor
`M` with `1 < d`, `d·M = N` — e.g. `N = 1` — there are no oldform generators, so
`cuspFormsOld N k = ⊥` and the LHS `⊥ ⊓ _ = ⊥ ≤ anything`. (For `N = 1`: `IsOldformGenerator`
needs `1 < d ∧ d·M = 1`, impossible.) **Discharged by `bot_le`** after showing `cuspFormsOld = ⊥`.
Note: this is subsumed — the step case's generator analysis vacuously holds when there are no
generators, so an explicit base case may be unnecessary; keep it for clarity.

**Step (N, assuming the result for all M < N).** Take `f ∈ cuspFormsOld N k ⊓ charSpace χ`.
1. χ-slice the SPAN (not the element): use `cuspFormsOld_iSup_inf_charSpace` is the wrong handle
   here. Instead `span_induction` on `f ∈ cuspFormsOld N k` with a predicate carrying the
   charSpace membership is also wrong (charSpace not preserved by individual generators). **Correct
   handle:** `f` is a finite sum of generators `f = Σ_j c_j · V_{d_j}(g_j)`, `g_j ∈ S_k(Γ₁(M_j))`,
   `1 < d_j`, `d_j·M_j = N` (unfold `Submodule.mem_span_iff` / `Submodule.span_induction` to get
   the finite generator combination). [L1]
2. For each generator `V_{d_j}(g_j)`: split `g_j = oldPart_{M_j}(g_j) + newPart_{M_j}(g_j)` via
   `cuspFormsOld_isCompl_cuspFormsNew` at level `M_j`. By `levelRaise` linearity,
   `V_{d_j}(g_j) = V_{d_j}(oldPart g_j) + V_{d_j}(newPart g_j)`. [L4]
3. **New piece** `V_{d_j}(newPart g_j)`: `newPart g_j ∈ cuspFormsNew M_j k`. χ-slice it via
   `exists_finsupp_charSpace_of_cuspFormsNew` at level `M_j`: it is a finsupp sum over characters
   ψ of `(cuspFormsNew M_j k ⊓ charSpace_{M_j} ψ)`-components. Apply `V_{d_j}` (linear). The
   ψ-component lands in charSpace at level N lifted by `levelRaise_mem_cuspFormCharSpace`. Since
   the *total* `f ∈ charSpace χ` and the lifted pieces sit in independent χ-isotypic components
   (`cuspFormsOld_iSupIndep_inf_charSpace`), only ψ lifting to χ survives — others contribute 0
   to the sum. For the surviving ψ: by the **conductor fact**
   (`conductor_theorem_dichotomy_cuspForm_strong`), a nonzero χ-form that is a `V_{d_j}`-raise from
   level `M_j` forces `χ` to factor through `N/d_j = M_j`, i.e. `m_χ = χ.conductor ∣ M_j`. Hence
   `V_{d_j}(newPart g_j ⊓ charSpace ψ)` is a generator of `cuspFormsOldChar N k χ m_χ` via
   `levelRaise_cuspFormsNew_le_cuspFormsOldChar` (needs `m_χ ∣ M_j`, `M_j ≠ N` since `1 < d_j`,
   `d_j·M_j = N`, `newPart g_j ⊓ ψ ∈ cuspFormsNew M_j k`). ✓ lands in `cuspFormsOldChar`. [L3]
4. **Old piece** `V_{d_j}(oldPart g_j)`: `oldPart g_j ∈ cuspFormsOld M_j k`. Also `oldPart g_j ∈
   charSpace_{M_j} (χ restricted/descended)` — needs `oldPart` commutes with diamond + the
   χ-descent of g_j (g_j inherits a character at level M_j because `V_{d_j} g_j` is in charSpace χ
   at level N; pin it down by the conductor theorem's Case A bundle, which descends g_j to level
   M_j *in the lowered charSpace*). Apply the **INDUCTION HYPOTHESIS at level `M_j < N`**:
   `oldPart g_j ∈ cuspFormsOld M_j k ⊓ charSpace_{M_j} ≤ cuspFormsOldChar M_j k χ_{M_j} m_χ`. So
   `oldPart g_j = Σ_i V_{e_i}(h_i)`, `h_i ∈ cuspFormsNew (M'_i) k`, `m_χ ∣ M'_i`, `M'_i ≠ M_j`,
   `e_i·M'_i = M_j`. Apply `V_{d_j}`: `V_{d_j}(oldPart g_j) = Σ_i V_{d_j}(V_{e_i}(h_i))`. **NOW use
   `levelRaise` associativity** (L2): `V_{d_j}(V_{e_i}(h_i)) = V_{d_j·e_i}(h_i)`, a single V-raise
   from level `M'_i` to level `N` (since `(d_j·e_i)·M'_i = d_j·(e_i·M'_i) = d_j·M_j = N`). With
   `m_χ ∣ M'_i`, `M'_i ≠ N` (as `M'_i ≤ M_j < N`), `h_i ∈ cuspFormsNew M'_i k`, this is a
   generator of `cuspFormsOldChar N k χ m_χ`. ✓ lands in `cuspFormsOldChar`. [L2 + L5]
5. Sum over j, i: a finite sum of `cuspFormsOldChar` elements is in `cuspFormsOldChar` (submodule
   closed under +, scalar). ∎ [L6]

**Why strong induction terminates:** every recursive call is at level `M_j` with `1 < d_j`,
`d_j·M_j = N`, so `M_j = N/d_j < N`. Well-founded on `<` on ℕ.
